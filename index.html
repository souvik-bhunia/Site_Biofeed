The Solution

Converting discarded food
into premium animal nutrition

BioFeed processes urban post-consumer food waste into highly nutritious, shelf-stable feed pellets suitable for poultry, aquaculture, and livestock — closing the nutrient loop.

✓
Lower Feed Costs
Significantly cheaper to produce than conventional corn or soy-based animal feed, reducing farmer input costs. Feed accounts for 80% of total livestock production expenses.
✓
Nutritionally Promising
Pilot-scale testing shows good protein (7.7 g/ml Batch 1) and carbohydrate profiles. We're actively refining nutrient supplementation to meet full industry benchmarks.
✓

Moisture reduced through a structured drying process, using jaggery as a natural preservative and binding agent — achieving optimal 10% moisture content.

Methodology

How BioFeed Is Made

A four-stage process transforms diverse urban food waste into standardized, commercial-grade animal feed pellets.

01
🏗️
Collection & Sorting
Post-consumer food waste is gathered from local urban sources — hostels, restaurants, and cafeterias — and sorted by type to ensure consistent quality input. Debris is removed prior to processing.
02
🌡️
Processing & Drying
Waste is cleaned, dried in a hot air oven at 50–80°C to reduce moisture content to 10%, then ground into a uniform powder. This prevents microbial growth and extends shelf life significantly.
03
💧
Formulation
The powdered waste is blended with jaggery (a natural preservative and binding agent), ginger as a natural antibiotic, and powdered eggshells as a calcium source — creating a balanced feed mixture.
04
🌿
Pelletisation & Curing
The mixture is shaped into uniform feed pellets in sizes of 2–5mm suited for different animals, then undergoes a final drying stage to achieve a stable, shelf-ready product.
